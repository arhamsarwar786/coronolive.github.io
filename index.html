How Can I Protect Myself?

There is currently no vaccine to prevent coronavirus disease 2019 (COVID-19). The best way to prevent illness is to avoid being exposed to virsus & protect yourself.

Regular
Handwashing

Regular hand washing and sanitizing is considered the most important precaution to reduce the chances of contamination from the virus lingering on surfaces. Wash your hands for a minimum of 20seconds at every point in time.

Using a facemask
helps prevent
contacting the virus

Facemasks can stop you from breathing in coronavirus from an infected person. They must be worn properly for the best results.

Social Distancing

What do I need to Know?

What is Coronavirus?

Coronaviruses are a family of viruses that cause disease in animals. Seven, including the new virus, have made the jump to humans, but most just cause cold-like symptoms. Covid-19 is different from these other two coronaviruses in that the spectrum of disease is broad, with around 80 per cent of cases leading to a mild infection. There may also be many people carrying the virus

How did the outbreak start?

The source of the coronavirus is believed to be a "wet market" in Wuhan which sold both dead and live animals including fish and birds. The animal source of Covid-19 has not yet been identified, but the original host is thought to be bats. Bats were not sold at the Wuhan market but may have infected live chickens or other animals sold there. Bats are host to a wide range of other viruses including Ebola, HIV and rabies.

Is Corona in Nigeria?

Unfortunately, Yes. On the 27th of February 2020, the Federal Ministry of Health confirmed a coronavirus disease (COVID-19) case in Lagos State, Nigeria. The case was an Italian citizen who works in Nigeria and returned from Milan, Italy to Lagos, Nigeria on the 25th of February 2020. From that time till now, the of number positive cases in the country have continued to increase, resulting in a partial shutdown of gatherings and businesses around Nigeria.

How is corona spread?

Like cold and flu bugs, the virus is spread via droplets when a person coughs or sneezes. The droplets land on surfaces and are picked up on the hands of others and spread further. People catch the virus when they touch mouths, noses or eyes with infected hands.

Is it airborne?

There are some reports about how long the virus can survive on different surfaces, these are not confirmed but it safe to advise that we wipe down surfaces with disinfectants as often as we can. The current advice is that the disease can only be spread between close contacts – defined as spending more than 15 minutes within two metres of an infected person.

How does Social distancing help?

Social distancing is important because coronavirus spreads when an infected person coughs small droplets - packed with the virus - into the air. These can be breathed in or can cause an infection if you touch a surface they have landed on, and then touch your face with unwashed hands. This means the fewer time people spend together, the less chance there is of this happening. We can do this by self-isolation. This means staying at home and not leaving it for any other reason apart for exercise. Please don't go to work, school or public areas during this time.

Is there a cure for the coronavirus?

There is no specific treatment, although doctors are trialling existing drugs for viruses such as Ebola, malaria and HIV. Early results seem promising but, until full clinical trials have been concluded, doctors cannot be certain that the drugs are effective. Work to develop a vaccine is accelerating but it is unlikely to be available until next year.

I have a cough. Should I get tested?

If you have a wet cough or you’re sneezing, you probably have a cold or an allergy. If you have a dry cough, a fever and difficulty breathing, you should get tested.

I think I have been infected. What do I do?

If you feel unwell and notice symptoms like fever, cough and difficulty in breathing, please call NCDC’s 24/7 toll-free number 08009700000010 and self-isolate while waiting for directions. Please do not self-medicate as it might be dangerous to your health.
